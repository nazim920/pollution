Interactive Air Quality Analysis

Task:

Developing an interactive visualization tool to analyze air quality across regions and cities, focusing on key pollutants like PM2.5, PM10, and NO2.

Operations:

  • Visualize pollutant levels across different regions.
  • Select pollutants to focus on specific data points.
  • Explore city-level data using zoom.
  • Examine historical trends with a timeline slider.
  • Download datasets for in-depth analysis.

Details:

Data Requirements:

Regional air quality data (PM2.5, PM10, NO2) and GeoJSON for country boundaries.

Algorithms:

  • Data preprocessing for pollutant mapping.
  • Outlier detection to ensure data accuracy.
  • Interactive tooltips to enhance user experience.

Metrics:

  • Regional pollution averages.
  • Normalized pollution levels based on student density.

Features:

  • Color-coded maps to represent PM2.5, PM10, NO2 levels.
  • Trends over time with historical data visualization.
  • Interactive tooltips to provide detailed pollutant information.

What the Visualizations Show

The visualizations display the concentration levels of key air pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, NO2) across different regions and cities. Users can explore how pollution levels vary over time and across geographic locations, with color-coded maps and tooltips providing additional contextual data.

Who the Visualization is For

This tool is designed for students studying environmental science, researchers, policymakers, educators, and health officials who are concerned about air quality and its impact on public health and the environment.

Why It Matters

Air pollution has serious health consequences, especially for vulnerable populations such as children, the elderly, and people with respiratory conditions. By providing an interactive and accessible platform, this visualization helps raise awareness and encourages actions to mitigate pollution levels.

How to Read the Visualization

Each region on the map is color-coded based on the level of a specific pollutant. Darker colors indicate higher pollution levels. You can select pollutants for comparison, zoom in on specific cities, and explore trends over time using the interactive timeline. Hovering over a region will display detailed pollutant levels and additional contextual data.

  • Hover over regions to view pollutant levels.
  • Toggle between different pollutants for comparison.
  • Zoom in to explore data at the city level.
  • Adjust the timeline slider to view historical data trends.

Key Highlights

Focus areas include:

  • Multi-pollutant visualization for comprehensive analysis.
  • Historical trends analysis for understanding pollution changes over time.
  • Health impact insights to guide public health policies and initiatives.
